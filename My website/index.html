My approach to hair is both deliberate and instinctive — a balance between structure and spontaneity. Each project begins with a vision, but it’s the process that defines the final outcome. I treat hair like a living material, shaping colour and texture the way a designer works with form and light.

I’m drawn to contrast — soft pastels against bold lines, deep rich tones beside subtle texture. My work sits between precision and expression, where every placement feels intentional, yet never overworked. It’s a form of art that moves, evolves, and exists beyond the chair.

Inspiration comes from everywhere — architecture, fashion, design, photography, the people I meet. I see my practice as part of a wider creative landscape, where colour becomes another language of storytelling and identity.

I want every client to leave feeling like themselves, but elevated — as if they’re wearing something crafted, not styled. My aim is simple: to create work that feels charismatic, suitable, and undeniably eye-catching.

This space represents where I am now — exploring the intersection of editorial, education, and collaboration, while shaping a more independent and defined vision. I want my work to make people stop, look, and think: wow.
